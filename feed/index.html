Where the Big Bucks are going Next,

And How to Prepare.

Stock templates:  If you like to save time and get pre-made designs for your invoices and quotes, this website is for you. However, I don’t usually like to download these templates and justly simply enter my information. I like to treat them as a base then work with them to get a design that fits best with my brand. There are hundreds of templates to chose from – but “Good artists copy. Great artists steal.”(Pablo Picasso) Simply copying work is not healthy and should never be done. If you don’t make your business look unique, you will look simply like the rest.

Building your website:  A lot of influencers suggest a particular company for starting your own website. However, through the years I’ve almost tested them all and the one I find to have the best support and reliability is Bluehost.  Something I love about them is they rarely have maintenance and have almost instant support. Plus their servers are built to handle WordPress framework, which I use to build all of my websites. If you are on the fence of a hosting company I would highly recommend Bluehost.

Utilize Social Media: Social media is free advertising on steroids. This is one avenue I’m working to dig into progressively. Every post you make on these platforms can be leveraged to show the world your work and likely may lead to future work.

Business Cards: In the creative industry you run into a lot of people who try to mix things up with their business cards. However, I’ve found minimalist business cards work the best. The main purpose of business cards is to transfer your contact details to someone else, why not make it easy. Keep your business title and name in a design-friendly text, then on the opposite side keep it clean with your logo in the centre.

Video is power: Youtube is the 2nd largest website visited in the world. Having content on a site that pulls in huge volumes of visitors every day is a goldmine to get noticed. However with the platform being saturated with many other creators, using tools like Tubebuddy will significantly increase your chances of becoming founded and recognised. In 2009 I start putting content on Youtube and saw average growth – posting videos weekly with low view counts. Starting in February 2017 I tried out Tubebuddy and have been getting massive volumes of people flooding to my channel. Tubebuddy in my books is a game changer.

New York City

The adventure started by car.

As we travelled 780km towards New York City from Toronto, Canada. Totalling in around 7 hours of driving, we arrived at our first Airbnb in Jersey City with a rooftop patio that overlooked lower Manhattan. After unloading we spent the next 24 hours exploring the city and capturing content of what New York is known for. Without a thought of ever piecing our trip together in video format, upon arriving back in Toronto – I put my SD into my MacBook and noticed there was a story here that could be told.

Over the next week I spent hours organizing clips, color grading, souring sfx/music to make a piece of work that embodies our experience in New York City. This video was shot handheld on the Sony A7s with a Canon 24-105 lens. I’ve left some pictures below and the finally video from the trip. If you have any burning questions hit me up on my socials.

Toronto is such a diversity city,

there is always something new to see.

The Mission

Toronto is such a diversity city, whether you are in the east or west end there is always something new to see. The idea behind this video sparked from the emptiness of content online of high quality drone footage of different regions of Toronto. I saw the shores of Humber Bay to be my first region to hit, as they offer a mix range of views – from boats, to condos, to beaches to bridges – the list goes on.

Scouting the location and finding the best times to shoot was first on my hit list. Planning every shot was essential as the battery on the phantom 4 is short, you have to make every second count.  I started by looking on google maps and going into the 3D mode to get a perspective of the best shoot locations in the Humber bay area. I found going to the Humber Bay Arch Bridge and Humber Bay Park West would offer the best vantage points. After two weeks, and 5 flights later – I had collected enough footage to compile a video together.

Why I love this drone

New angles, new moment and new ideas. I never fully realized the endless possibilities that a drone offers. It has become one of the most essential pieces of equipment I use to film now and has brought new life to my filmmaking process, where the boundaries are not restricted and brings the “wow” factor to every project I create.
