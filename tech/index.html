Technology Used

A summary

The most overwhelming aspect of becoming a filmmaker is learning and understanding the mass amounts of tech that is associated with the job. There are hundreds of different options when it comes to cameras, lens, tripods, filters, stabilizers, microphones etc. And the unfortunate part is none of it is very affordable - that makes the buying decision not easy. Whether you are making a documentary, music video, short film, travel edit or something as simple as a how-to video you need to work out the tech, which will accommodate your project goals. Often more than not - working with the wrong tech, or too much tech can burden the filmmaking process.

One thing that often gets into people heads is its all about the tech, and if you dont have the most expensive camera with a red ring around your lens then you might as well give up. This is simply NOT true. I originally started shooting video on the Canon T3i (with kit lens) and one thing is for sure it did not have 60fps at 1080p, very good autofocus or low light performance. A good example of a YouTuber who absolutely embellishes the fact that he uses a low-end camera is Simon Cade - heres an example of one of his videos shot all the Canon T3i. The point is that having a lot of equipment is a luxury, not a necessity, which will help ease the filmmaking process - with less room for error. That being said over the past six years Ive been successfully able to earn a modest amount of income through making videos online and for real world clients, whether that be for short promo videos, real estate, event recaps or music videos. Ive chosen to invest in a collection of tech, with the goal in mind that it will enhance my ability to create more interesting films. Its taken me six years to get my kit down to something that I am comfortable with and confident. I owe a sum of my filmmaking knowledge of to blogs such as nofilmschool.com, phililipbloom.com and friends through my university degree who have got me to where I am today.
